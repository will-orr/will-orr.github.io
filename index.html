Hello! My name is Will Orr. I am a PhD candidate at the Annenberg School of Communication where I study the sociotechnical dynamics of creating and evaluating Artificial Intelligence systems.

My research uncovers the cultures, practices, histories, and epistemologies that shape emerging AI technologies. My goal is to excavate the assumptions underpinning these technologies to critically assess how these systems can be designed and deployed more responsibly. My work primarily engages interpretive, qualitative approaches, such as interviews, ethnographic, and archival methods, though I am also trained in computational and quantitative methods and welcome collaborations that integrate these approaches.

I am currently completing my dissertation, which explores how data practitioners enact and negotiate the concept of data quality in the creation of AI datasets. It uncovers the messy and contingent work of maintaining quality in the shifting landscape of contemporary AI and data science.

Prior to the PhD, I completed a Bachelor of Arts from the University of Melbourne, where I studied sociology, politics and international relations, and economics. I also earned an Honours in sociology, and a Master of Applied Data Analytics from the Australian National University (ANU). I also worked as a research assistant at the Justice and Technoscience (JusTech) lab at ANU's School of Regulation and Global Governance (RegNet).

My work has been published across academic journals and conferences, and has been featured in news outlets such as Australian Broadcasting Corporation Radio, Canberra Daily, and Nature.
